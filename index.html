Introduction Of BlockChain

Blockchain is a decentralized, distributed ledger technology that ensures secure and transparent record-keeping of transactions across a network of computers. Originally developed as the underlying technology for Bitcoin, blockchain has evolved to support a variety of applications beyond cryptocurrencies. It operates on a system where each transaction is recorded in a "block," which is then linked to the previous block, forming a "chain." This structure ensures that once a transaction is added, it cannot be altered or deleted, providing a high level of security and immutability. The decentralized nature of blockchain eliminates the need for intermediaries, reduces the risk of fraud, and enhances the efficiency of transactions. Its potential applications span various sectors, including finance, supply chain management, healthcare, and more, making it a transformative technology with wide-ranging implications.

Components

These are the Components

Genesis Block

The first block in a blockchain. Hardcoded into the blockchain protocol.

Main Blocks

Regular blocks that are added to the blockchain after the genesis block. Each main block references the previous block.

Candidate Blocks

Blocks that are in the process of being mined or validated. Once validated, they become part of the main blockchain.

Full Nodes

Store the complete blockchain ledger, validate transactions and blocks, and enforce all the consensus rules of the network.

Light Nodes

Download only the block headers rather than the entire blockchain, which allows them to verify transactions.

Mining Nodes

Participate in the creation of new blocks by solving complex mathematical problems (proof-of-work).

Public Ledger

A public ledger is open to anyone and everyone can participate in the network.

Private Ledger

A private ledger is restricted to a specific group of participants.

Hybrid Ledger

A hybrid ledger combines elements of both public and private ledgers.

Proof of Work (PoW)

Miners compete to solve complex mathematical puzzles to validate transactions and create new blocks.

Proof of Stake (PoS)

Validators are chosen to create new blocks and validate transactions based on the number of coins they hold and are willing.

Proof of Authority (PoA)

A limited number of approved validators (authorities) are responsible for validating transactions and creating new blocks.
